About  the  Event

Hack the Heat is a Hackathon created by and for high school students in the Twin Cities metro area. Running from August nth to nth 2016, beginner and advanced participants alike will arrive and stay for 24 hours, working away to create something awesome. Whether you've never written a line of code or you're a published app developer, Hack the Heat has something to offer for everybody.

An event like no other.

FAQ

  • Q: What is a "hackathon"?

    Hackathons are large events in which people come together to engage in collaborative computer programming or hardware modification for an extended period of time.

  • Q: How long will the event last?

    Hack the Heat will run for 24 hours, from noon to noon.

  • Q: What should I bring?

    Bring your computer and a mouse. If you plan on sleeping, bring a sleeping bag, pillow, and toothbrush. Desktop PCs and monitors in addition to laptops are allowed.

  • Q: Where will I sleep?

    Those who plan to sleep can do so anywhere throughout the venue. Make sure to bring a sleeping bag and pillow.
